Zoho CRM audit memo: downturn read from Nov 2025

Based on 1,000,000 audit rows from Nov 2024 to Mar 2026. This is a CRM-behaviour analysis, not a direct revenue analysis.

Bottom line: The downturn is real in the audit logs. The clearest break starts in November 2025, not January 2026. The strongest root-cause signal is a collapse in lead creation and prospecting activity, especially in TON. EB then shows weaker engagement quality. SMB underperformance is likely real commercially, but this audit file is too shallow to explain it cleanly.

2) What likely broke first

The cleanest leading indicator is human-created leads. Lead adds were stable in Aug-Sep 2025, spiked in Oct 2025, then fell off a cliff from Nov 2025 onward. The biggest single driver was Printo Management lead creation.

  • All human lead adds: Aug 279, Sep 258, Oct 1953, Nov 212, Dec 227, Jan 132, Feb 184
  • Printo Management lead adds: Aug 216, Sep 195, Oct 1905, Nov 131, Dec 128, Jan 62, Feb 75

4) Diagnostic tree

  • Question 1: Did lead supply or lead allocation drop from Nov 2025?
    • Check campaigns stopped / imports stopped / assignment rules changed / TON owners changed.
    • If yes, that is the primary break.
  • Question 2: Did EB narrow to fewer active reps or fewer real meetings?
    • Check rep-level MOM frequency, active accounts per rep, stale opportunities, and touch freshness from Nov onward.
    • If yes, that is the second-order break.
  • Question 3: Is SMB actually a demand/conversion issue outside CRM?
    • Check store walk-ins, quote count, quote-to-order, lost reason mix, category inquiry mix, and average order value by store.
    • If yes, the root cause sits outside this audit file.

5) CEO actions for the next 7 days

  1. Audit exactly what changed in lead generation / routing between Oct and Nov 2025.
  2. Pull rep-wise TON lead creation, touches, and conversion for Aug-Feb.
  3. Pull EB rep-wise MOM count, active accounts, and stale deals from Nov onward.
  4. For SMB, stop using audit logs as the main diagnostic and switch to footfall, quotes, category inquiries, and lost reasons.
